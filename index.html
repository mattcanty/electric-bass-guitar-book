Freddie Thompson

Testimonial by professional bass player

My bass playing background is primarily steeped in Gospel music. I first picked up the bass at the age of 13. After about four years of playing I was regarded as possessing a certain amount of raw talent. However, I knew I did not really have solid technique and that, if I was to pursue bass playing seriously, this had to be addressed.

In 1982 I purchased a copy of Laurence Canty's Electric Bass Guitar. It transformed my approach, my confidence in navigating around the instrument and my playing through any chord changes. Later, I had the opportunity to have lessons with Laurence. He taught me how expand on what I had learnt from the book, and how to practise reading music.

30 years later, with a career involving many tours and recordings, I decided it was time to revisit my technique and make sure it was as solid as it should be. I'd read many instructional books over the years, but there was one that was the foundation of my technique - Electric Bass Guitar. I knew I wouldn't still have the original book, so I browsed the internet and ordered the new and updated edition.

It has been a pleasure just to go through the exercises again, discovering where I had acquired bad habits, and addressing them. Also, remembering Laurence's simple but very effective way of teaching scales and chord studies, gave me the confidence to pursue my own journey. To me this book is the bass instructional book.

Freddie Thompson has worked with the likes of Westlife, M People, Deniece Williams, Gloria Gaynor, Chaka Khan, George Michael, Tom Jones and J-Lo to name but a few.

Yolanda Charles

Foreword by Laurence Canty's ex-pupil

I met Laurence when I had been playing bass for a year. During our relaxed lessons, he shared with me his approach to becoming a good bass player and it's all here in his book. I've found knowledge of theory and the construction of chords invaluable. My technique, developed with Laurence's guidance, has helped me to avoid any physical problems such as RSI. Attention to the actual sound I was making, note-length, string muting and how the right hand influences the overall sound, have added much to my playing and musicianship. This awareness has helped me succeed as a professional musician.

The book starts by emphasising the importance of understanding the instrument and how to practise comfortably. It explains the patterns and 'shortcuts' to enable you to get around the neck, and become 'gig ready' within weeks. The more advanced player will find notated exercises, harmonic ideas and technique suggestions, which all add to a greater understanding of the bass.

Colin Greenwood

Foreword by Laurence Canty's ex-pupil

I've been playing bass with Radiohead since its inception. I'm into the space a bass can occupy between the drums and the harmonies of guitar and voice, and how the instrument can become part of the soul of the band. After recording 'The Bends' in 1994, I decided that I wanted to find out more about the instrument. I was lucky enough to have a series of lessons with Laurence, using his book 'Electric Bass Guitar'. The book helped me to understand the freedom that harmony and chord theory can give a bass player. It also helped reinforce and develop what I already knew about rhythm and technique. One of the books many strengths is that it can be used by any player, at any level; it is a very clear, concise introduction to the instrument. It has been repeatedly useful to me over the years, across several tours and albums, in fact, the bass line on Airbag (the first track of 'OK Computer') was the result of a lesson on chord theory with Laurence. I'm still using this supportive, challenging, and confidence-inspiring book for tuition and reference.
